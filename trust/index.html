Trust

"Do your best and trust God with the results"- Jeremy Lin

Tito

Jun 25, 2018 • 2 min read
"Do your best and trust God with the results"
- Jeremy Lin

I feel like God read my last blog post and is now saying “ok... let’s see if you TRULY trust me as your source of comfort”

So here I am doing a trust fall with God..

I moved in with a host family today and our ”shower” is just a bucket for cold water
The toilet doesn’t flush and after an almost-diarrhea bowel movement... I needed to ask the 6 year-old boy how to “flush” the toilet.
He goes “oh you just pour this other bucket of water into the toilet... if you needed help using the bathroom, just ask”

I then texted Deb, Melissa, and Sarah for advice on my current situation...
I was extremely blessed by the number of parents (especially the mothers, you know who you are) that were on the family mission trip as they  literally flocked to the sick / weak and offered a million different medications and solutions to help alleviate the suffering.


It dawned on me that I ran out of "drinkable" water and the host family I stayed with didn’t get water from Associacion Vida and so my best bet was to get water from the local store. Thankfully the “house” I was staying at (the majority of the house is outside) was basically attached to the back of a store.

I knock on the backdoor of the store; they open and I ask for water; I then proceed to enter the little store (behind the counter because this is a street facing store with a small gap for customers to exchange money for products) and it’s literally just me and the 2 employees (male and female) in the area of the store where typically only employees stand.

The male looks at me like I'm crazy ... because I’m a Spanish speaking Asian who looks fresh off the boat with American style clothes and 2 empty water bottles asking to buy their biggest container of water.

They show me a 600ml and a 650ml and I calculate which one is cheaper per mL (frugal as always) and ask for 6 bottles of the cheaper one.
Less than $2 for 6 bottles.
I then proceed to swallowing as many healthy meds as I can (to a safe extent)

Dinner is in a kitchen that’s using an echo stove.
I’m pretty sure I ate a few ants in my dinner because they just crawled in my food.. oh well
This is the real Guatemalan experience...
As raw as it gets.

As for the computers...

Axel, his tech team, and I discussed and prayed over the potential ideas and use cases for these computers.

We have the computers and the resources and the desire to teach. But we currently have no one to teach...

How do we get the word out about these classes?
Axel and Edi recommended using a Radio, facebook, and even flyers around the town.

The lack of planning on their part is making it very challenging for both parties. But at this point, if I don’t trust that God will make a way for me... what am I really doing here?
