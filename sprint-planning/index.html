What is a Sprint? A sprint is a focused dash towards the completion of the immediate goals. Usually short-term duration to allow for more improvement and reflection. Thus establishing an efficient and productive workflow sooner rather than later.

Sprint Duration: ~2 weeks (recommended, but ultimately up to you to find the most effective duration that also fits your timeline / schedule)

Sprint Planning Questions


1. Review quarterly objectives and key results (OKRs)

Take the time to pull up your OKRs and look at our current progress. Update OKRs if needed.

2. Create Sprint Board

To display all the content from planning session

3. What are your ongoing priorities for the next sprint?

What are your marching orders for the next sprint? What do you need to get done?

4. What tasks are we going to take on to complete these priorities or goals in the next sprint?

Now that we know what your strategic direction and goals are, break these into discrete tasks for the next two weeks.

Sprint Retrospective

How was this sprint?

How do you feel about the sprint?

What were your goals in this sprint?

What did you initially set out to accomplish?

What did you accomplish?

What did you set out to accomplish and succeeded at?

What unplanned work did you accomplish?

What unplanned work did you accomplish and succeed at?

What did you fail to accomplish?

What did you set out to accomplish but failed at? Why?

What did you descope or deprioritize?

What tasks, projects, or initiatives did you either (a) descope or (b) deprioritize? Why?

What is the single most important thing you worked on or accomplished?

Share the most valuable thing you worked on or contributed to during this sprint

What can you do to make the next sprints better and more effective?

How can you make things better for the future?

Sprint Show-and-Tell

Show off a substantive work product that is awesome, interesting, or useful that you accomplished during the sprint. Find someone to show/tell that can hold you accountable

Sprint Tips

  1. Start before you have all the answers, and then be creative in finding solutions to obstacles you encounter.
  2. Constantly self-reflect and adapt. Spend a few minutes each day planning, reflecting, and asking, "What's standing in my way?"
  3. Work toward clearly defined short-term goals. Breaking big goals into short-term goals that are time-boxed will help create a sense of accomplishment and move you forward.
