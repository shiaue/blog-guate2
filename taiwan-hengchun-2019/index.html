Oh what a whirlwind these past 2 weeks have been...

Week 2: Medical Work

Heng Chun Experience

The second week was more challenging for me but I think it really humbled me.
It ought to give you enough disappointments to make you humble and break your spiritual pride.

Areas of Improvement:

  1. I felt like the Taiwanese language barrier was difficult to surpass. I know God's love has no bounds and He works through language barriers, but it was still difficult for me. The selfish part of me wanted to be more "useful". The part of me that trusts God fully knows that "it isn't about me". Serving with this ministry isn't about my feelings and my utility. I believe it's about humbly responding to God's calling, picking up the phone without an attitude, and abiding in who He says I am.
  2. Visiting the elderly in hospice, remote homes, and retirement homes really touched me because I don't usually interact with the elderly. Their stories, tears, and worn eyes gave me a genuine glimpse of Taiwanese history.
    It ought to teach you how to turn your mourning into dancing, your sadness into joy, and your sorrow into laughter. I am glad I was able to be a part of their stories.
  3. My favorite activity in Heng Chun was the indigenous school program that sang and danced for us. I really connected with those students and I hope I can return to that school.
  4. One of the biggest challenge of this week was not knowing and preparing for what we would do. It wasn't very clear to me how different all the activities were until we did them all.
  5. I didn't know the mission of the second week. If the mission is relational evangelism, I think that picking one activity and doing it all week would allow us to serve a specific need of the community as well as love a specific group of people enough for them to see Jesus. If the mission is education, I think the TAs could prepare to be better suited to educate on the chosen topic (oral cancer, hygiene, ALDH2). I think better communication of the plan, mission, and expectations for this week would have been invaluable for the team.
  6. I personally learned about Taiwanese culture in ways that I wouldn't have any other way. I really appreciated the opportunity to connect with the Mackay students on this week and I hope the trip continues to allow that opportunity. Engaging with the elderly and children also gave me another opportunity to understand Taiwan more. From the indigenous school to the remote home visits, I am humbled and grateful to be a part of this experience.

Takeaways

  1. God is Love. Love can involve leaving the home country, learning a new language, working late nights, not seeing loved ones for months, saving every penny, and above all it requires faith.
  2. It will be easier to connect to some believers than others and it is not a function of how much faith one has.
  3. Language barriers are like the Great Wall of China: built so people can't scale them, but sometimes it's ok to just enjoy the view from distance.
