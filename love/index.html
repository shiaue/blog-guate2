The original plan was just to focus on teaching teachers for maximum effectiveness but now we may have to teach students because we don't have enough teachers that are available for classes.

We have 10 students and they are all eager to learn. Edi and I went to tour schools and churches looking for students and teachers who are interested in technology and ¬†learning it. The contrast between these "labs" and what I have grown accustom to in the US feels like I time traveled. I feel like I am a part of history here.

At the church across the street I met Brian who was an eager learner and English teacher. As I described the mission to teach tech and to bring eager minds together I could just see Brian and another church member's face light up. Learning something, let alone something as difficult as technology (difficulty relative to Patzun), is brutal to do alone; so why not collaborate. I've been preaching about making a tech meetup here in Patzun where anyone with interest can come learn, build, and share resources and ideas. Although a pretty common idea in the US, the reactions I get here in Patzun are as if I whispered them a Nobel Prize worthy idea.

I really am planting seeds here... It's definitely not what I envisioned, but starting a technology revolution in a 3rd world city is not something you can just look up on wikihow.
