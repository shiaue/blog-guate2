Why Guatemala?

  1. Willow Creek Community Church has a partnership with Association Vida, a clinic in Guatemala. Having witnessed this partnership firsthand, I want nothing more than to be a part of it and to help strengthen the relationships between Willow Creek and Guatemala.
  2. I was absolutely blown away by Guatemala last month when I went as a high school leader. (Read more about the Phase 1 trip here)
  3. Guatemala will only fuel my passion for Spanish. (see below for more on Spanish)

The medical clinic could use my assistance and I was able to learn about the technology education, or lack thereof, in Guatemala.

The Mission

Teach: I am partnering with local organizations to create a computer lab with the goal to bringing over 30 laptops (Donated from local organizations and friends) for students. As much as I would love to teach Guatemalan students directly, I believe it would be more effective and sustainable to teach Guatemalan teachers and professors. This will allow for consistent communication and minimize the risk for the teaching to be lost as students become adults. My goal is to teach the professors how to maintain the laptops as well as developing a curriculum that makes it easy for Guatemalan students to learn but also challenges them to learn more like the American college system did for me.

There currently aren't any working computer science education opportunities for the students in Guatemala.
The government has a weekly program that is expensive and only provides students with 30-60min of computer time once a week!
(No one can learn from so little computer time)

The premise behind this mission is to expose the Guatemalan students to programming and give them the opportunity and resources to learn. (Just like I was given in college)

Serve: I will implement a cloud based solution to synchronize patient records at the the medical clinic. Currently, there is nothing to connect the patient data from the clinic, laboratory, and pharmacy. I am hoping to use their internet and a software solution to connect the patient database.
Also, I will also help translate for the Willow Creek Family Mission trip that will be there at the same time.

Love: When I am not teaching or working at the clinic, I will do everything I can to find extravagant ways to love the Guatemalans from the church community to my host family and evenyone in between. Again, Bob Goff says it best, "When we are fearlessly engaging with world with a child-like faith, it gives those around us the green light to do so too."

Implementation

To avoid the possibility of taxes, tariffs, confiscation, suspicion, or questioning from the Guatemalan customs (all possible outcomes from bringing one luggage full of laptops across international borders)

The plan is to distribute these laptops among the family mission trip members to get them across the border. An adult bringing 1-2 laptops shouldn't raise any eyebrows.

Once in Guatemala, laptops will be configured for their purpose.
This trip kicks off on June 18, 2018.
