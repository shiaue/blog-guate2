Sacrifice

" I guess that's just part of loving people: You have to give things up. Sometimes you even have to give them up."

Tito

Jun 26, 2018 • 3 min read
"I guess that's just part of loving people: You have to give things up. Sometimes you even have to give them up."

Daniel is the little boy. The woman might be Luiza's sister.

If you want to really put yourself in the shoes of others, you have to hear their story.

Today at dinner, 6 year-old Daniel was having an attitude and I saw the true colors of the family as we began to converse about real life in Patzun.

Just like how when an orange is pressed, squeezed, stressed - orange juice comes out. I believe what comes out when we are pressed reveals who we truly are.

Here are the deets on my host family.

Luiza (let's call her "grandma")
Glenda ("mom")
Aura ("aunt")
Daniel ("son")

Luiza's sister, who passed away, had 2 daughters (Glenda and Aura). Glenda has one son Daniel. Glenda's husband was an abusive alcoholic that passed away (not sure on how exactly). So technically she's the grandaunt of Daniel.

Aura and Glenda both work in the capital (Guatemala City) one of the most dangerous cities in Latin America. They have a group of 5 that sticks together, looks out for each other, and loves each other as they make the daily 03:00 commute to Guatemala City. These five warriors basically go into a war zone every day just to make it, just to survive. The commute is dangerous as buses are held up at gunpoint and and resistors are usually shot / killed without justice being served. Glenda and Aura have been mugged many times before and have learned to just give up whatever they had on them. They don't carry smartphones or a lot of money on them so if a bus is held up they have nothing to lose. The commute is unlike America because you can't sleep or even rest... you are always on the lookout for threats and shooters. Aura and Glenda return home to Patzun around 20:00 absolutely spent.

Daniel... Oh Daniel. The poor boy has no discipline or respect because his aunt and mother are rarely home. Luiza is burnt out, elderly, and tired of dealing with Daniel‚Äôs disrespect. Because no one is at home to discipline Daniel, he runs amok and has no self-control. Especially since he grew up with an abusive father, he seeks attention from anyone who can give it to him. He's too young to understand the loving sacrifices that his mother and aunt make on his behalf and just wants someone to play with.

Glenda sacrifices time with Daniel and being a part of his upbringing to put food on the table and allow Daniel to buy whatever clothes and toys he needs.

Ultimatums: No work available in Patzun; employment opportunities are available ¬†in the dangerous capital city. Higher education is very expensive and only high quality in the capital city and maybe Chimaltenango; there is no guarantee that you will be able to find specialized work with a degree afterwards, so why bother paying for school.

I give up warm showers for Lent... yet here they warm up water on an echo stove (like the ones we built in Poaquil) just to put it in a bucket for me to shower with. I feel like an alien in a different world, but I am doing my absolute best to put myself in the shoes of those here in Patzun.
