Morning

There is only one indoor basketball court (known as "the dome") in the city of Patzun and its only open once a week. These hoops are also the only hoops I saw in all of Patzun with actual basketball nets.

I think organized / pick-up basketball are more common than futbol here in Patzun because the city doesn't have the money or resources to build and maintain a futbol pitch. Basketball is much easier because all you need is a ball and a backboard/rim; no maintenance required.

Given my past love for the sport, I thought it would be interesting to take a look at the games going on in "the dome". Basketball in Guatemala is definitely a little bit different US hoops-I watched a men’s league tournament and it was WILD. No plays; didn't see any pick-n-rolls; back-doors; defensive traps / zones; or any crazy handles. I'm curious as to why this facility is only open once a week...

Evening

I attended church service with Carlos at 17:00 and it was very different. Lot's of chanting during worship. They had maybe 3 or 4 worship parts separated by announcements, sermon, introduction, and something else.

After church, my host family took me to eat gringas (tortilla, cheese, meat) from a street vendor (risky.. I know).
Manuel, Irma, Gladys, Brenda, and I just sat on stools and laughed until we had to go to bed.
